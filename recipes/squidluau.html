Squid Luau

Pronunciation: "Squid" "loo-ow" ("ow" pronounced in the way of expressing pain)

Yeah I know, but it's better than it looks trust me

Depending on preference and who makes it, it can be a savory dish with a nice sweetness to it

Ingredients

  • Squid
  • Taro leaves
  • Coconut milk
  • Hawaiian Sea Salt
  • Sweet yellow onion
  • Butter
  • Sugar

Steps

  1. Clean the taro leaves thoroughly and remove the leaf from the stems
  2. Chop the leaves into small pieces, like the size of kit kat bars
  3. Cut the onions into thin slices
  4. Boil the leaves in a pot of water with the Hawaiian Sea Salt, bring to a simmer and leave alone for 1 hour
  5. Clean the squid: remove feeler tentacles, innards, skin on the head, cartilage, beak, and discard
  6. Cut squid into bite size pieces and set aside
  7. Drain the leaves with a colander and push down to remove as much water as you can
  8. In a separate pot, melt the butter and saute the squid and onions until onions are translucent
  9. Throw in the leaves, sugar, salt, and coconut milk and mix it well
  10. Let it simmer for 30 minutes stirring occasionally
  11. Remove from heat

To Serve

  1. Grab a bowl and put rice on one side of the bowl
  2. Drop a good amount of the squid luau next to it
  3. Have at it!
