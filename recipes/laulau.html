Pork Lau Lau

Pronunciation: "l-ow l-ow" ("ow" pronounced in the way of expressing pain)

Lau lau is a polynesian dish that consists of taro leaves and meat (pork and butterfish), wrapped in ti leaf

Traditionally it is cooked in an underground oven (an imu)

However, most people now steam it on the stove

Ingredients

  • Pork butt and/or pork belly
  • Butterfish
  • Taro leaves
  • Ti leaves
  • Hawaiian sea salt

Steps

  1. Wash the ti leaves and taro leaves thoroughly
  2. Cut the stems of the taro leaves off, cut them into 1 inch long pieces, and set leaves aside
  3. Remove the stiff part of the stem on the ti leaf to make them more flexible
  4. Chop the pork and butterfish into 1 inch cubes (leave the fat!)
  5. Season meat with Hawaiian sea salt
  6. Place a few (3-5) taro leaves on each other, biggest at the bottom
  7. Add a handful of meat to the center
  8. Fold the taro leaves in (like a present) and pack it as tightly as you can
  9. Lay 2 ti leaves down in a plus shape and place the taro wrapped meat in the center
  10. Wrap the ti leaves in and tie the stems together to prevent it from falling apart (you may use butcher string if you can't tie it)
  11. Arrange all your laulau into a steamer and steam for 4 hours
  12. Remove laulau from steamer when done

To Serve

  1. Take one laulau and remove the ti leaf (you can't eat it)
  2. Place laulau on a bed of rice and break open with knife or fork
  3. You can add chili water on top if you would like
  4. Have at it!
